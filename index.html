Personal information

I consider that there are three moments in my history that influenced me to be responsible, resilient and strive for excellence.

The first of these is the childhood. As the son of a public school teacher and a military, I grew up learning to value studies and discipline to achieve goals ethically. Since childhood, I like to read books, to study, to dedicate myself to sports and to play the guitar. Period in which I believe I learned to aim for excellence, without harming others.

The second is the time I lived abroad. Due to my father's career mission, I lived in British Guyana for two years and had to drop out of high school in Brazil to live in an unknown country, without friends and with a language and culture different from Brazil. Despite the difficulties, I managed to adapt to the new environment and traditions. Not only did I adapt and overcome difficulties, going to high school abroad, being part of the National Volleyball and Tennis team, but I also developed as a person, learning to value the importance of diversity of thought and cultures in an environment.

The third and last is the preparation period to be approved at IME. Even moving abroad, I did not abandon the dream of studying engineering at an excellent university in Brazil and I continued dedicating myself to this goal while living abroad and after returning to Brazil. I find the act of learning rewarding and I enjoy teaching what I know to others.
